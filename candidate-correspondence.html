Responses from London Elections Candidates 2024

Save Our Safer Streets wrote to the main candidates for mayor and local and London-wide assembly members and asked them to agree to three pledges:

  1. To support LTNs in principle, if they are subject to robust engagement and consultation, implemented well and the monitoring data shows that they are succeeding in their objectives;
  2. To encourage the Mayor of London to work with boroughs to roll out more LTNs and intervene if there are proposals to remove successful LTNs so that TfL money isn’t wasted and the London transport plan isn’t put at risk;
  3. To push for your national party to include a pledge in their next election manifesto to fund more LTNs and reinstate the national guidance hastily withdrawn by Rishi Sunak so that there is a presumption for retaining LTNs in order to protect public health and avoid public money being wasted.

The responses we've received as of April 29 2024 are below.

We're pleased to say that many of the candidates standing for elections are supporting our aims and have policies that will help make our streets safer, with cleaner air.

Conservative

Hello,

Thank you for contacting me about low-traffic neighbourhoods (LTNs).

As you know, local councils are responsible for traffic on their roads, including implementing measures such as LTNs. I recognise that LTNs were intended to make residential streets safer for pedestrians and cyclists, notably by restricting cars and vans from using them as shortcuts. However, I also recognise that there has been concern that some LTNs are overly restrictive and/or or do not meet the needs of local people.

With this in mind, I welcome the draft statutory guidance the Government has published for councils, setting out that they must gain buy-in from local residents, businesses and emergency services when considering implementing new LTN schemes.

It is worth highlighting that a recent review suggested only 13% of people have responded to councils' planning consultations on LTNs, and just 18% feel that their views have influenced council decisions. As such, I see the new guidance as a positive step in terms of making sure councils engage local people before significant changes are made.

Thank you again for taking the time to contact me.

Thanks,

Freddie Downing
Conservative Candidate for City and East
