'Call in' Liveable Streets cabinet decision to Overview & Scrutiny Committee


To email the Overview and Scrutiny Committee and 'call in' Liveable Streets cabinet decision click here.

Act today - Help us get the mayor’s decision scrutinised! Over 200 of you brilliant people emailed your councillors last week to ask them to support Option 2. Now we need you to mobilise once again, today or tomorrow, to get the mayor’s dreadful decision on Liveable Streets properly scrutinised.

Tower Hamlets Council’s Overview and Scrutiny Committee can “call in” a decision made by the Leader or Cabinet but not yet implemented. They consider whether the decision is appropriate and have the power to recommend that it be reconsidered. While a decision is called in, the decision is on hold and the mayor can't begin to enact it. The committee will also have a chance to bring in witnesses to scrutinise if the mayor's decision has been made in the right way.

In order to kick-start this process, we need to email councillors and ask them to “call in” the decision. At least five of them need to put their name to the “call in” request for it to be accepted.

Please email your Labour and Green councillors ASAP to get this decision called in.

Subject: Please request for the Liveable Streets Cabinet decision from 20 September 2023 to be called in to the Overview & Scrutiny Committee

Introduce yourself and let them know how you felt about the mayor’s decision to go with Option 1 (removing almost all of the Liveable Streets schemes)

Points you could make in your email:

  • The decision was undemocratic as it ignored the stated views of the majority of local people, as shown by TWO the consultation results
  • The reasons given by the mayor for his decision at the meeting were incorrect, misleading or irrelevant. He relied on anecdotes and provided no hard evidence to support his decision.
  • For example, the claim that “Emergency Services want the schemes removed” is false. The Fire Service has not said this. The Metropolitan police have said exactly the opposite. They want the schemes to stay to help reduce anti-social behaviour.
  • The Officer Report on which the decision was based was full of errors and omissions.
  • It did not include key information such as correspondence from Barts NHS Trust and Transport for London, both strongly in support of retaining the schemes.
  • The report author described the consultation process as “flawed”, which does not imply a fair and objective process.
  • Removing the schemes will cost over £2.5m of public money. This kind of spending requires more robust justification.
  • Ask them TODAY to request that the decision be called in to the Overview and Scrutiny Committee. The call-in has to be made by at least five councillors by 5pm on Friday 29 September, so time is tight.

Please email all the Labour and Green councillors on Monday or Tuesday if you can. THANK YOU!
